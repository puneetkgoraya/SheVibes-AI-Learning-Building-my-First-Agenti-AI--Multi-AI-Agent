Backlog Prism Concierge:

A multi-agent AI pipeline that transforms raw, unstructured requests into fully documented Scrum-ready backlog items — automatically.

What it does:

Product Owners spend significant time converting messy, informally written requests — from Slack messages, emails, meeting notes, and Jira entries — into properly structured backlog items. Backlog Prism Concierge automates that mechanical work.
A raw request enters the pipeline in any form — formal or informal, brief or detailed, emotional or clinical, native or non-native English. Six specialised AI agents process it sequentially, each doing one job precisely. The result is a fully documented Jira backlog item with a normalised request, classification, user story, acceptance criteria, test cases, and story point estimate — ready for sprint planning.
The Product Owner remains in the loop at every critical decision point. The agents handle the mechanics. The human handles the judgment.

Why it is useful: 

Effective backlog items take time and skill to write. Most organisations have more requests than capacity to process them properly. Ideas get lost, misrepresented, or never make it to the backlog at all. Junior team members and informal communicators are disproportionately affected — their requests are harder to process and easier to overlook.
Backlog Prism Concierge solves this by giving every request — regardless of how it was written or who wrote it — the same quality of processing and the same standard of representation in the backlog.

How it is built:

Built on Zapier's AI Agent platform using Google Gemini as the AI engine, with Jira as the pipeline state machine and output destination, Confluence as the organisational knowledge base, and Slack as the primary intake channel.
No code. Prompt engineering, context engineering, and integration architecture in plain English.

Status:

Active development. Currently completing Sprint 1 — single channel intake, normalisation agent, and Jira integration.
